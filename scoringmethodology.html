My Stocks — Scoring Methodology

How each stock's BUY / HOLD / SELL recommendation is computed, and how the 3-tier autotrader uses those scores
113+ stocks scored daily 30+ signals · 0–100 scale Refreshed 8:30 AM ET Mon–Fri Autotrader executes 9:35 AM

How It Works — At a Glance

Step 1
Fetch Price History
~260 days of daily OHLCV bars from price_history table (sourced from Alpaca)
Step 2
Compute Indicators
50/200 SMA, EMA 9/21/50, RSI 14, MACD 12/26/9, volume ratio, 52W range, cross events
Step 3
Merge Fundamentals
PE, P/S, EPS/revenue growth, ROE, D/E, dividend, analyst consensus from Finnhub + Yahoo
Step 4
Apply Signal Weights
30+ signals each add or subtract points from a base of 0
Step 5
Clamp 0–100
Raw sum floored at 0, capped at 100. Write to stock_signals
Step 6
Classify
≥50 = BUY · 10–49 = HOLD · <10 = SELL
The score is additive and transparent — every point in the "Why?" column traces directly to a signal below. There are no hidden multipliers or black-box weights. The "Why?" column in the dashboard shows every signal that fired, sorted by |impact|.

Step 1 & 2 — What Gets Computed

Moving Averages

SMA 50 and SMA 200 — simple averages of the last 50 and 200 closing prices. Used for golden/death cross detection and price-vs-MA comparison.

EMA 9, EMA 21, EMA 50, EMA 200 — exponential moving averages that weight recent bars more heavily. EMA 9/21 are short-term swing signals. The EMA stack (price > EMA9 > EMA21 > EMA50) is the strongest bull-alignment signal.

Golden Cross / Death Cross

When the 50-day SMA crosses the 200-day SMA:

  • Golden cross (50 crosses above 200) — most powerful long-term buy signal. Institutions watch this.
  • Death cross (50 crosses below 200) — long-term bearish signal.

The system scans back up to 60 sessions to find the most recent cross date.

RSI — Relative Strength Index (14-day)

  • RSI < 30 — deeply oversold: sharp recent drop, mean-reversion candidate
  • RSI 30–45 — recovering from oversold: momentum turning up — the most actionable entry zone
  • RSI 45–60 — neutral-bullish: healthy uptrend
  • RSI > 70 — overbought: stretched, pullback risk

MACD — Moving Average Convergence Divergence (12/26/9)

Compares a 12-day EMA vs 26-day EMA; signal line is a 9-day EMA of the difference.

  • Bullish cross — MACD line crosses above signal line: strongest momentum signal
  • Above signal (sustained) — upward momentum continuing
  • Below signal — downward momentum

The system records how many sessions ago the last bullish cross occurred (up to 6 sessions back).

Volume Ratio

Today's volume divided by the 20-day average volume (excluding today to avoid self-reference). A ratio ≥ 1.5× on an up day signals institutional buying. The same ratio on a down day signals heavy selling.

Fundamentals (from Finnhub + Yahoo Finance)

  • Trailing / Forward P/E — valuation vs sector average; if fwd P/E < trailing, earnings are accelerating
  • P/S ratio — price/sales; used as PE fallback when no earnings
  • PEG ratio — PE ÷ EPS growth rate (Peter Lynch metric): PEG < 1 = undervalued vs growth
  • EPS growth (3Y CAGR) — from Finnhub, in % form (18.5 = 18.5%)
  • Revenue growth (3Y CAGR) — from Finnhub
  • ROE — return on equity: proxy for business quality (Buffett metric)
  • Debt/Equity — balance sheet risk
  • Dividend yield — annual dividend as % of price (Finnhub primary, Yahoo fallback)
  • Short float % — % of float sold short (Yahoo Finance)
  • Analyst consensus — Finnhub buy/sell/hold counts + Yahoo recommendationMean (1=Strong Buy → 5=Strong Sell)
  • Price targets — Yahoo Finance targetMeanPrice / targetHighPrice / targetLowPrice
ETFs and funds skip all fundamental signals (P/E, PEG, EPS growth, ROE, D/E, short float, analyst ratings). Only price-based technical signals and dividends apply to ETFs.

Step 3 & 4 — All Signal Weights

Each signal fires independently. Signals that don't apply (e.g. ETF trying to fire a PE signal) score 0. Starting score = 0. All signals are cumulative.

Step 5 — BUY / HOLD / SELL Thresholds

After clamping the raw sum to 0–100:

▲ BUY
Score ≥ 50
Multiple strong signals aligning. Momentum, trend, and ideally fundamentals all pointing up. High conviction to add or initiate a position.
● HOLD
Score 10–49
Net positive signals but not strong enough to add aggressively. Maintain existing position. Watch for signals to strengthen or weaken.
▼ SELL
Score < 10
Net bearish. Signals dominated by negative factors. Consider reducing or exiting. Not a panic signal — the score may recover next session.
Why 10 as HOLD/SELL boundary? Below 10 means the net arithmetic sum of all signals is close to zero or negative — the stock has almost nothing going for it technically. A score of 10+ means at least some meaningful bullish signal is present.

Autotrader — 3-Tier Entry & Exit System

When Autorun is ON, the system executes trades autonomously at 9:35 AM ET each trading day. A trade only executes if all three tiers agree. One veto from any tier blocks the trade. The 8:30 AM digest always includes recommendations (evaluate only, no orders) regardless of Autorun state.

Tier 3 — Outermost Gate
Market Regime Filter
Uses SPY's pre-computed signals from stock_signals — no extra data fetch needed. This gate is all-or-nothing: if it fails, no new buys run at all. Exit logic always runs regardless.
  • BULL — SPY above 200DMA and above 50DMA → proceed to Tiers 2 and 1
  • CAUTION — SPY above 200DMA but below 50DMA → correction in progress, block all entries
  • BEAR — SPY below 200DMA → bear market, block all entries
  • UNKNOWN — SPY not in stock_signals (not in watchlist) → block all entries (safe default)

Why the 50DMA gate? SPY dropping below its 50DMA signals a correction in progress — even if the 200DMA hasn't broken yet. Buying individual stocks during an index correction is high-risk: even great stocks get sold off in broad market drawdowns. The 50DMA gate catches this early before it becomes a bear market.

Important: SPY must be in your watchlist for this gate to work. If SPY is absent from stock_signals, regime = UNKNOWN and all entries are blocked.

Tier 2 — Quality Filter
Watchlist-Only · Not Overbought · Not Overextended
All conditions must pass — one failure blocks this stock entirely:
  • Symbol is in personal watchlist (is_active=1) — Discover universe stocks are never auto-traded
  • Score ≥ 50 on the full 30+ signal engine
  • Price ≥ $5 (no penny stocks)
  • RSI ≤ 65 — blocks entries when a stock is already overbought
  • Price ≤ 50DMA × 1.08 — blocks entries when price is >8% above the 50DMA

Why the >8% extension block? The 50DMA acts like a rubber band — stocks tend to revert to it after a strong run. When a stock is already 8%+ above its 50DMA it means: (1) you'd be buying after most of the move, (2) traders who bought earlier start taking profits creating natural resistance, and (3) if it reverts to the 50DMA, that's an 8%+ drop — straight into your hard stop territory. The safer entry is to wait for the stock to pull back toward the 50DMA, or hold flat long enough for the 50DMA to "catch up" to the price.

Example: TWLO score 97 but 12% above its 50DMA ($137 price vs $122 50DMA) → blocked. Even with a great score, the risk/reward at that extension is poor.

Tier 1 — Entry Gate
High-Conviction Entry + Guardrails
All conditions must pass:
  • Score ≥ 65
  • ≥ 2 technical confirmations out of 4:
    • RSI between 30–65 (not oversold, not overbought — momentum entry zone)
    • MACD = bullish or above-signal line
    • Price above 50DMA
    • Today's volume ≥ 1.3× 20-day average
  • Not currently held (one position per symbol — no averaging down)
  • Portfolio has open slots (max 8 positions; configurable in system_config)
  • Sufficient buying power: 20% cash buffer always reserved; 50% of remaining deployed
  • No earnings within next 5 calendar days (Finnhub calendar)
  • Market hours: 9:35 AM – 3:45 PM ET

RSI confirmation window (30–65): The RSI gate inside Tier 1 is deliberately narrower than the Tier 2 hard block (≤65). RSI below 30 is deeply oversold — the stock may still be in freefall. RSI above 65 means the move is already extended. The 30–65 window targets stocks that have started recovering but haven't run too far — the best entry zone for a swing trade. Stocks with RSI 65–75 can still be bought if the other 3 confirmations (MACD, above 50DMA, volume) provide 2 of the needed signals.

Exits are evaluated before entries on each run. All current Alpaca positions are checked — including those opened manually.

Rounding edge case: if floor(qty × 0.5) = 0 (e.g. holding only 1 share), the full position is sold rather than 0 shares.

Position Sizing Formula

  • cashBuffer = accountEquity × 0.20 — always keep 20% uninvested
  • deployable = max(0, (buyingPower − cashBuffer) × 0.50) — deploy 50% of remaining
  • maxPerPos = accountEquity × 0.10 × vixMultiplier — max 10% per position, VIX-adjusted
  • perSlot = min(deployable / openSlots, maxPerPos)
  • shares = floor(perSlot / currentPrice)
  • If shares < 1 → skip (insufficient funds)

VIX position size multiplier (estimated from VIXY price in price_history):

VIX levelMultiplierEffect
Below 20 (calm)1.00×Normal position size
20–30 (elevated)0.75×Size down 25%
Above 30 (high volatility)0.50×Size down 50%

VIXY must be in your watchlist for VIX sizing to work. If absent, multiplier defaults to 1.0× (normal size).

Autotrader Record-Keeping

Every order (buy or sell) is written to the autotrader_trades table with: symbol, action, qty, exit_reason, alpaca_order_id, executed_at. This table drives the "days held" calculation for the time stop.

Order Types

  • All autotrader orders are market orders during regular hours (9:35–15:45 ET)
  • No extended hours, no limit orders in autorun mode
  • No short selling, no options
