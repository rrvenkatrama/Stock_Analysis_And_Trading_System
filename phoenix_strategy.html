Two Strategies, One Portfolio

The My Stocks dashboard runs two independent auto-trading engines simultaneously, each managing its own cash pool (50% of portfolio equity each). They operate on the same Alpaca account but track positions separately via a strategy tag in autotrader_trades.

Strategy A
⚡ Alpha
  • Buys quality stocks at technically optimal moments
  • RSI in healthy range, MACD bullish, above 50DMA
  • 30+ signal composite score ≥ 65
  • Can buy anywhere on the 52wk range
  • Hold: days to weeks (swing trade)
  • Exits on signal deterioration or 30-day time stop
Strategy B
🔥 Phoenix
  • Buys fundamentally excellent companies at deep price discounts
  • Deliberately buys when technicals look bad (fear discount)
  • Requires 40%+ below 52-week high
  • Earnings and revenue growth still intact
  • Hold: weeks to months (recovery trade)
  • Exits on fundamental deterioration or 60-day time stop

⭐ Confluence Signal — Highest Conviction

When a stock receives a BUY from both Alpha and Phoenix simultaneously, that is a rare high-conviction signal. It means the stock is: (1) technically set up for a move right now, AND (2) deeply discounted with intact fundamentals. These stocks are highlighted in gold in the dashboard Stocks table and receive priority in both autotrader queues.

Example: A stock 45% below its 52-week high, EPS growing 25%, RSI just recovering to 42, MACD turning bullish — this stock qualifies for both Alpha (technical setup) and Phoenix (fundamental value). That combination is rare and worth acting on aggressively.

Phoenix Philosophy

Markets regularly over-punish stocks due to fear, macro narratives, sector rotation, or short-term earnings misses — even when the underlying business remains strong. The Phoenix strategy deliberately targets these situations.

The name reflects the thesis: companies beaten down by fear, not by fundamental damage, that recover and rise again. The key insight is distinguishing a value trap (cheap for good reason — business is deteriorating) from a genuine opportunity (cheap due to sentiment, business intact or growing).

What Phoenix looks for

  • Price dislocation: stock is ≥40% below its 52-week high — deep enough that fear, not fundamentals, is driving it
  • Year-over-year decline: price today is below price 1 year ago — confirms sustained downtrend, not a recent blip
  • Earnings intact: positive EPS growth — the business is still making more money, not less
  • Revenue growth: top line still expanding — growth story not broken
  • Value on forward metrics: forward P/E or P/S below sector average — you are paying less than peers for the same earnings
  • Management confidence: stock buybacks signal insiders believe the stock is undervalued at current prices
  • Analyst support: even after the drawdown, analysts still see upside — consensus hasn't turned negative

What Phoenix deliberately ignores

  • RSI level — it will be low, that's the point
  • MACD — will likely be bearish or neutral during the accumulation phase
  • Whether the stock is above or below its 50DMA — it's almost certainly below
  • Short-term momentum — we are buying before the recovery, not after
NOW (ServiceNow) as the archetype: a company with 25%+ EPS growth, strong ROE, active buybacks, fortress balance sheet — yet down 40-45% from its 52-week high due to AI disruption fears and broad SaaS selloff. The business hasn't changed. The price has. Phoenix buys that gap.

Entry Gates — All Must Pass

Phoenix applies hard gates before scoring. A stock failing any gate is excluded entirely, regardless of score.

Hard Gate 1
Deep Price Dislocation
  • Current price ≤ 52-week high × 0.60 (i.e., ≥40% below 52-week high)
  • Current price < price 1 year ago (year-over-year decline confirmed)

The 40% threshold is intentional. A 20% drawdown can happen in any normal correction. 40%+ signals genuine fear-driven overselling — the rubber band is stretched far enough that mean reversion becomes a high-probability thesis.

Hard Gate 2
Earnings Growth Intact
  • EPS growth (3-year trailing) > 0% — earnings are still growing, not contracting
  • Revenue growth (3-year trailing) > 0% — top line still expanding

This is the value-trap filter. A stock can be cheap and getting cheaper if the business is deteriorating. Positive EPS and revenue growth confirm the company is still on an upward trajectory — the price drop is sentiment, not fundamentals.

Hard Gate 3
Valuation Check
  • Forward P/E < sector average OR Forward P/S < sector average
  • (If neither P/E nor P/S available: skip this gate — some growth companies don't have earnings yet)

Not enough to be cheap on absolute terms — must be cheap relative to peers. This ensures we're buying genuine value, not a sector that is expensive across the board.

Minimum Score Gate
Phoenix Score ≥ 60

After passing all hard gates, the stock must score ≥ 60 on the Phoenix scoring engine (see below).

Exit Conditions

Phoenix holds longer than Alpha — exits are fundamentals-driven, not technical. The strategy is designed to tolerate continued price weakness while the fundamental recovery thesis plays out. Time stop is 60 days (vs 30 for Alpha).

Exit Rule — Hard Stop
Price −15% from Entry → 100% Sell

Wider than Alpha's −8% because Phoenix intentionally buys into weakness. A stock can dip further before recovering. However, a −15% move from entry price (on top of the 40%+ drawdown already priced in) suggests the thesis may be breaking. Full exit — no partial.

No RSI or MACD exits for Phoenix. These signals will look bad throughout the holding period — that's expected. Selling on a bad RSI would defeat the entire strategy.

Position Sizing & Cash Partition

Each strategy manages 50% of total portfolio equity. Alpaca does not support sub-accounts, so the partition is virtual — tracked via the strategy column in autotrader_trades.

Cash pool calculation (per strategy)

  • strategyEquity = totalEquity × 0.50
  • strategyHeld = market value of positions tagged 'phoenix' in autotrader_trades
  • strategyBudget = strategyEquity − strategyHeld
  • cashBuffer = strategyEquity × 0.20
  • deployable = max(0, (strategyBudget − cashBuffer) × 0.50)
  • maxPerPos = strategyEquity × 0.10 × vixMultiplier
  • shares = floor(min(deployable / openSlots, maxPerPos) / price)

Confluence override: When a stock has BUY from both Alpha and Phoenix, both strategies may attempt to buy it. The first strategy to execute wins the position (tagged to that strategy). The second strategy skips it (already held). This prevents doubling up on a single name accidentally.

Screening Universe

Phoenix screens from the full watchlist (where complete fundamental data is available from Finnhub). Stocks without Finnhub fundamentals (EPS growth, ROE, D/E) cannot be scored and are skipped.

To expand the Phoenix universe: add stocks to your watchlist. The morning refresh (8:30 AM) pulls full fundamentals for all watchlist stocks, which Phoenix then scores at 9:35 AM.

Schedule

  • 8:30 AM ET: phoenix_screener.scoreAll() runs after morning refresh → results in daily digest email
  • 9:35 AM ET: phoenix_autotrader.run() executes alongside Alpha — exits first, then entries

Dashboard

  • Stocks table — Phoenix signal column added next to Alpha signal. Gold row highlight when both = BUY.
  • Phoenix panel — dedicated section showing all Phoenix BUY/WATCH candidates with scores, drawdown, EPS growth, buybacks
  • Autotrader history — Strategy column shows Alpha or Phoenix for every trade
