System Overview

StockTrader v1 is a swing trading assistant that runs continuously on a local mini PC. It scans a curated universe of 30 liquid US stocks twice per day, scores each one across four dimensions, and produces a prioritised buy/hold/exit plan for your approval. No trade is ever placed without you clicking Approve.

Core Philosophy

  • Human in the loop — every trade requires explicit approval. The system advises; you decide.
  • Swing trading horizon — hold periods of 3–10 days, targeting 5–15% moves.
  • Quality over quantity — max 4 open positions from a screened universe of 30 large-cap, liquid stocks.
  • Anti-churn — never swaps a position unless a replacement scores 20+ points higher.
  • Market-adaptive — thresholds automatically loosen in high-fear markets (VIX > 40) to surface oversold opportunities.
  • Paper-first — defaults to Alpaca paper trading. Go live only after 2–4 weeks of consistent paper results.

Plan Contents

Each plan contains one or more of the following action types:

BUY — New position

  • Symbol, share count, estimated cost
  • Suggested entry price (current market price)
  • Target price (analyst PT or ATR-based)
  • Stop loss (5% below entry, auto-placed)
  • Risk/reward ratio and estimated hold duration
  • Top 6 signals driving the recommendation

HOLD — Keep existing position

  • Current gain % since entry
  • Distance remaining to target
  • Reason (winning, momentum intact, no better alternative)

EXIT — Close position

  • Triggered when: within 1.5% of stop loss, score fell below threshold, or a significantly better stock is available
  • For swaps: shows which stock will replace this one
⚠️
Market hours enforcement: Orders are only placed between 9:35 AM and 3:45 PM ET on weekdays. Approving a plan outside market hours saves the approval — execution happens at the next market open check.

Composite Scoring System

Every stock receives a composite score from 0–100. 50 = neutral (no signals either way). Scores below 35 are weak, 50–65 are moderate, 65+ are strong. In normal markets the threshold to become a candidate is 55. In extreme fear (VIX > 40) it drops to 35 automatically.

Probability Estimate

Each candidate also gets a probability of success estimate (35–85%). It is deliberately conservative — never exceeds 85% — and is derived from the composite score with boosts for strong technical setups and penalties for earnings proximity. Both the score and the probability must exceed their respective adaptive thresholds for a stock to qualify.

Indicators & Signals

Technical Indicators (35% weight)

Baseline score starts at 50 (neutral). Bullish signals add points; bearish signals subtract.

RSI — Relative Strength Index (14-day)
Measures momentum. The system specifically looks for oversold conditions as swing entry opportunities.
RSI < 25 → +15 pts (deeply oversold) RSI < 35 → +10 pts (oversold) RSI 35–50 → +4 pts RSI > 70 → −8 pts (overbought)
Golden / Death Cross (50MA vs 200MA)
Long-term trend indicator. A golden cross (50MA crossing above 200MA) is one of the strongest bullish signals.
Just crossed golden → +12 pts Golden cross active → +8 pts Just crossed death → −18 pts Death cross active → −12 pts
Price vs 50-day MA
Short-term trend. Price above 50MA = uptrend. Below = downtrend.
Above 50MA → +5 pts Below 50MA → −4 pts
Price vs 200-day MA
Long-term trend. Price above 200MA = secular uptrend. Major institutional reference level.
Above 200MA → +5 pts Below 200MA → −6 pts
MACD (12/26/9)
Momentum crossover. Bullish crossover (MACD line crossing above signal) indicates momentum turning positive.
Bullish crossover → +8 pts Above signal line → +3 pts Bearish crossover → −6 pts
Bollinger Bands (20-day, 2σ)
Volatility bands. Price at the lower band in a quality stock often signals a mean-reversion opportunity.
At lower band (oversold) → +6 pts At upper band (overbought) → −4 pts
Volume Ratio (vs 20-day avg)
Confirms conviction. High volume on a bullish signal means institutional participation; not just retail noise.
Volume > 2× avg → +7 pts (strong spike) Volume > 1.5× avg → +4 pts (elevated)
52-Week Low Proximity
Stocks within 10% of their 52-week low may represent value entry points when other signals align.
Within 10% of 52-wk low → +4 pts Used in combination with oversold RSI for high-conviction setups

Fundamental Indicators (25% weight)

P/E vs Sector Average
Compares the stock's price-to-earnings ratio against its sector benchmark. A stock trading at a P/E discount is considered relatively undervalued.
P/E < 70% of sector avg → +12 pts P/E < sector avg → +6 pts P/E > 150% of sector avg → −5 pts
EPS Growth (YoY)
Year-over-year earnings-per-share growth. Positive and accelerating EPS growth indicates a business gaining profitability.
EPS growth > 20% → +10 pts EPS growth > 10% → +6 pts EPS growth > 0% → +3 pts Negative EPS growth → −5 pts
Revenue Growth (YoY)
Top-line growth. Confirms the business is expanding, not just cutting costs to boost earnings.
Revenue > 15% → +7 pts Revenue > 5% → +4 pts Revenue declining → −4 pts
Debt/Equity Ratio
Balance sheet health. Low debt means the company can weather downturns and doesn't pay heavy interest costs.
D/E < 0.3 → +5 pts (low debt) D/E > 2.0 → −6 pts (high debt)
Earnings Date Risk
Upcoming earnings create binary volatility risk. The system penalises stocks close to reporting to avoid holding through unpredictable events.
Earnings within 5 days → −10 pts + prob −8% Earnings within 14 days → −4 pts (caution) Earnings > 14 days away → no penalty

Guardrails & Safety Rules

All rules are enforced in trader/guardrails.js before any order is placed. These cannot be bypassed by approving a plan.

  • 🕐
    Market hours only: Orders placed between 9:35 AM and 3:45 PM ET on weekdays only. Approvals outside this window are queued until the next eligible time.
  • 💼
    Max 4 open positions: Never holds more than 4 stocks simultaneously. New buys are blocked if all slots are full.
  • 📅
    Max 4 trades per day: No more than 4 new orders in a single trading day, preventing overtrading.
  • 💰
    Max 10% of account per position: With $100K account, maximum $10K per stock (~100 shares of a $100 stock). Prevents concentration risk.
  • 🏦
    20% cash buffer: Always keeps at least 20% of the account in cash. Never fully deploys all capital.
  • 🛑
    5% automatic stop loss: Stop-loss order placed immediately after every buy. If the stock drops 5% from entry, it sells automatically without needing approval.
  • 📆
    No same-day close: Positions opened today cannot be closed today — enforces the swing trading horizon and avoids wash-sale complications.
  • 🚫
    No short selling: Long positions only. No borrowing, no margin trading.
  • ⚡
    No options: Equity only. No derivatives of any kind.
  • 📊
    Earnings blackout: Will not buy a stock with earnings within 5 days. Earnings create unpredictable binary risk incompatible with swing trading.

Anti-Churn Logic

The system uses several rules to prevent unnecessary position changes. Stability is valued — each unnecessary trade incurs transaction costs and tax events.

Should we exit or swap an existing position?

Evaluated in order — first matching rule wins:

HOLD — Never exit if:
  • Position is up >3% AND still >2% from target → protect the winner
  • No replacement scores 20+ points higher
  • Position score is ≥60 with no better alternative
EXIT — Sell if:
  • Within 1.5% of stop loss → exit early to protect capital
  • Score collapsed below adaptive threshold AND a better stock exists (20+ point gap)
  • Score dropped below 45 → flag for review

The 20-Point Swap Rule

A new candidate can only displace an existing position if it scores at least 20 points higher on the composite scale AND the current position is flat or down. This prevents the system from churning the portfolio every time a marginally better stock appears.

Earnings Protection on Swaps

Even if a new stock passes all other criteria, it will not be added to the plan as a swap target if it has earnings within 5 days. The swap is skipped and the current position is held.

Position Sizing & Capital Allocation

How position size is calculated

  1. Buying power is read live from Alpaca at plan-build time (currently $200K on paper — 2× cash due to margin account structure)
  2. Deployable capital = buying power × 50% = $100K maximum per plan
  3. Open slots = max positions (4) minus currently held positions
  4. Per-slot budget = min(deployable ÷ open slots, account equity × 10%) = min($25K, $10K) = $10K per position
  5. Shares = floor($10,000 ÷ current price)

Stop Loss & Target

  • Stop loss: 5% below entry price, placed automatically as a stop order immediately after the buy fills
  • Target: Analyst consensus price target if within 30% of current price; otherwise entry + 3× ATR (Average True Range)
  • Risk/Reward: Minimum 1:1 expected; system prefers setups with R/R ≥ 2:1
  • Hold duration: Default 3 days; 7–10 days for golden cross setups with strong fundamentals
