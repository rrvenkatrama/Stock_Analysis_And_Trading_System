Rate Limit Management

  • FUND_DELAY = 1200ms between symbols (2 Finnhub calls/symbol → safe at 60 req/min)
  • QUOTE_DELAY = 800ms between Yahoo quote calls
  • Upgrades: max 20 stale symbols per run, 2000ms delay; weekly cadence
  • Yahoo Finance blocks IP after ~10 rapid requests; clears in 30–60 min; switch to Finnhub via sources.js when blocked

8. Auto-Trading System New Feature

The auto-trading system adds a 3-tier decision engine on top of the existing scoring system. It does not replace any existing functionality — scores, signals, and the dashboard all remain unchanged.
8:30 AM ET — autoEvaluate(false) → recommendations only, no orders
9:35 AM ET — autoRun()  [only if autorun_enabled='1' + market hours 9:35–3:45 ET]
  │
  ├── 1. Tier 3: Market Regime Filter
  │        ├── BEAR → log + email, skip buys, proceed to exits only
  │        └── BULL → proceed to exits and entries
  │
  ├── 2. Evaluate EXIT conditions for ALL Alpaca positions
  │        (includes manually opened positions)
  │        ├── Price 8%+ below entry                      → sell 100% (hard stop)
  │        ├── Score < 25                                  → sell 50%
  │        ├── RSI > 75                                    → sell 50%
  │        ├── EMA 9 crossed below EMA 21 (≤3d ago)       → sell 50%
  │        ├── MACD turned bearish                         → sell 50%
  │        └── Held ≥ 30 calendar days with no gain      → sell 50%
  │
  ├── 3. Check ENTRY conditions — watchlist only (is_active=1)
  │        ├── Skip: symbol already held (any qty > 0) → diversify instead
  │        ├── Tier 2: score ≥50, price ≥$5, RSI ≤65, not extended >8% above 50MA
  │        ├── Tier 1: score ≥65 + ≥2 technical confirmations + no earnings within 5d
  │        ├── Position sizing: cashBuffer=equity×0.20, deployable=(BP−buffer)×0.50
  │        └── Approved → place market buy → log to autotrader_trades
  │
  └── 4. Log all decisions to system_log → sendAutotraderEmail()

8.1 Autorun Toggle

Storage: system_config table · config_group='autotrader' · config_key='autorun_enabled' · config_value='1' or '0' · Default '0'

StateButton AppearanceOn Click
OFF (default)⏸ Autorun: OFF (gray, neutral)GET /autorun/toggle → flips to ON
ON▶ Autorun: ON (green, pulsing dot)GET /autorun/toggle → flips to OFF

Toggle route also: logs to system_log + sends mode-change email + redirects to /.

Timing rule: Turning Autorun ON triggers no immediate orders. Recommendations appear in the next 8:30 AM digest; execution happens at the next 9:35 AM cron. This gives the user time to review the 8:30 AM recommendations before any orders are placed.
First-run on Autorun ON: The next 8:30 AM run evaluates all current Alpaca positions — including any opened manually before Autorun was enabled — against the exit rules. No position is grandfathered in.

8.3 Tier 2 — Quality + Mean Reversion Filter

Universe restriction: The autotrader operates exclusively on stocks in the personal watchlist (watchlist table, is_active=1). Discover universe stocks, manually typed symbols, and any external source are never auto-traded. To make a stock eligible, add it to the watchlist first via "+ Add" or "+ Watch".
Tier 2 — Quality Gate (All Three Must Pass)
  1. Quality gate: Score ≥ 50 AND at least one of: ROE > 15%, EPS growth > 10%, div yield > 1.5%, PE below sector avg. Price ≥ $5 (no penny stocks). asset_type = 'stock' only.
  2. Oversold filter: RSI < 55 AND price not more than 5% above its 50-day SMA.
  3. Volume confirmation: Most recent bar volume ≥ 1.0x 20-day average.

8.4 Tier 1 — Entry/Exit Execution Rules

Tier 1 — Entry/Exit Execution

Entry Conditions (ALL must be met)

  1. Score ≥ 65
  2. At least 2 of: RSI 30–55 | price crossed above 50-SMA ≤3 sessions | MACD bull cross ≤5 sessions | volume ≥1.3x on up day
  3. No existing position in this symbol — if any shares are currently held in Alpaca (regardless of how they were opened), no additional shares are bought. Select a different stock to fill the slot instead.
  4. Not at max positions (default 8)
  5. Sufficient buying power after 20% cash buffer
  6. Not within 5 days of earnings
  7. Market hours 9:35 AM – 3:45 PM ET
Re-entry rule: Once a position is fully closed (Alpaca qty = 0), the autotrader may repurchase that stock on any future run if entry conditions are met again. There is no permanent blacklist.

Exit Conditions and Sell Quantity

Exits are partial (50%) by default to reduce false-signal impact and preserve upside on recoveries. The hard stop-loss is the only full exit.

ConditionShares SoldRationale
Price 8%+ below entry100% — full exitHard stop — capital preservation, no partial
Score < 2550%Signal deteriorating — reduce exposure
RSI > 7550%Overbought — take partial profit, let rest run
EMA 9 crossed below EMA 21 (≤3 sessions ago)50%Short-term reversal signal
MACD turned bearish (macd_trend = 'bearish')50%Trend momentum turning negative
Held ≥ 30 calendar days with no gain50%Time stop — trim dead weight, don't fully exit

Rounding: floor(currentQty × 0.5), min 1 share. If result = 0 (e.g. holding 1 share), sell the full position instead.

Manual positions: The autotrader evaluates and may sell positions opened manually via the Buy button, not only those it opened itself. Any position held in Alpaca is subject to exit rules once Autorun is ON.

Position Sizing

cashBuffer     = accountEquity × 0.20
deployable     = max(0, (buyingPower − cashBuffer) × 0.50)
maxPerPosition = accountEquity × 0.10
perSlot        = min(deployable / openSlots, maxPerPosition)
shares         = floor(perSlot / currentPrice)
// min 1 share; skip if perSlot < currentPrice

8.6 Autorun OFF Mode (Recommendation Only)

Default mode. Formalized behavior when autorun_enabled = '0':

  1. Full refresh runs normally (all phases)
  2. Auto-trader evaluates all 3 tiers but does NOT place orders
  3. Daily digest gains a "Recommended Trades (Autorun OFF)" section:
    • BUY recommendations: symbol / score / reason / suggested position size
    • SELL recommendations: held symbols with exit rationale
    • Footer: "Autorun is OFF — enable to execute automatically"

8.7 Autorun ON Mode (Auto-Execute)

When autorun_enabled = '1' — tradeJob cron fires at 9:35 AM ET (separate from the 8:30 AM morning refresh):

  1. autoRun() re-checks autorun_enabled='1' and market hours (9:35 AM – 3:45 PM ET) before proceeding
  2. Calls evaluate(true) — places real orders (vs evaluate(false) at 8:30 AM which only recommends)
  3. Check Tier 3 (market regime)
  4. Exits first: evaluate all current Alpaca positions (including manually opened ones) — place partial or full market sell orders per exit table above
  5. Entries: filter watchlist-only BUY stocks through Tier 2 → Tier 1 → guardrails → place market buys. Skip any symbol already held.
  6. All executed orders recorded in autotrader_trades table
  7. All decisions logged to system_log (source = 'autotrader')
  8. sendAutotraderEmail(results) — execution summary with sells/buys and ✅/❌ status
Concurrency guard: autorunning flag prevents multiple simultaneous runs, same pattern as refreshRunning in scheduler.

8.8 Trade Notifications (Email)

Daily digest addition (Autorun ON): "Today's Auto-Trades" section with:

  • Buys executed: Symbol | Shares | Price | Est. Cost | Top 3 signal reasons
  • Sells executed: Symbol | Shares Sold | % of Position | Price | Proceeds | Exit reason
  • Skipped (guardrail blocked): Symbol | Reason (e.g. "already held", "earnings in 3 days")
  • No trades: "No signals met all 3 tiers today"

Immediate error alert on any order failure: symbol, order details, error message.

Mode-change email on toggle: timestamp, mode entered, current positions list.

10. Known Issues & Constraints

  1. Finnhub /stock/price-target returns 403 (paid). Workaround: use Yahoo Finance yf.quote() fields targetMeanPrice/High/Low — free, zero extra calls.
  2. Yahoo Finance rate-limits after ~10 rapid requests — clears in 30–60 min. Mitigated by 800ms delay + withRetry() exponential backoff.
  3. VIXY proxy for VIX is approximate — VIXY × 1.8 + 2 ≈ VIX. Acceptable for regime filter.
  4. price_history stores ~380 days only — 2Y/5Y gains show "—" in table. Chart modal fetches from Alpaca on-demand.
  5. No intraday monitoring — auto-trader runs once at 8:30 AM. Stop-loss checked on next day's open, not intraday. Intentional (swing, not day trading).
  6. Alpaca paper mode default — set ALPACA_BASE_URL in .env to go live.
  7. MySQL JSON column gotcha — plan_json and reasons returned as already-parsed objects by mysql2. Always guard: typeof row.col === 'string' ? JSON.parse(row.col) : row.col.

11. Design Decisions (Do Not Reverse Without Discussion)

  1. Human control always available — Autorun OFF is the default and must remain functional. Toggle reachable in one click from header.
  2. Plain <a href> for navigation-critical actions — Toggle Autorun, Scan Now, Refresh Now, Add/Remove ticker. No JS onclick. Learned from production failures.
  3. No day trading — Minimum hold 1 trading day (no same-day close). Time stop at 30 calendar days with no gain (50% partial sell). Target: 5–20 day swing holds.
  4. No margin, no borrowing — instant settlement only. 20% cash buffer always enforced.
  5. Exit-first philosophy — in autotrader.run(), exits evaluated before entries. Freeing capital takes priority.
  6. All 3 tiers must agree — one tier veto blocks the trade. No partial overrides.
  7. Scoring engine unchanged — autotrader is a consumer of stock_signals. Existing 30+ signal engine runs unchanged; autotrader adds gating logic on top.
  8. Anti-churn — score drift (60→45) does NOT trigger a sell. Only sell at score < 25 or hard exit conditions. Prevents unnecessary churn and taxes.
  9. Paper mode safe for autorun testing — Autorun ON in paper mode places paper orders, not live. Validate behavior before switching to live.
  10. Watchlist is the autotrader's universe — the autotrader never trades a stock not in the personal watchlist. Adding a stock to the watchlist is the explicit human act of authorising the autotrader to consider it.
  11. One position per symbol, no averaging down — if a symbol is held (any qty > 0), the autotrader will not buy more regardless of score. A new slot must open first. Prevents concentration risk and forces diversification.
  12. Partial exits by default — selling half on non-stop-loss conditions preserves upside on recoveries. The hard stop (8% below entry) is the only full-exit trigger because capital preservation overrides upside preservation in a loss scenario.
  13. Re-entry is permitted — once fully out (qty = 0), a stock can be repurchased. No permanent blacklists. The scoring engine decides whether conditions warrant re-entry.
  14. Manual positions inherit exit rules on Autorun ON — the autotrader does not distinguish between positions it opened and positions opened manually. Once Autorun is ON, all held positions are subject to exit conditions at the next 8:30 AM run.
  15. No immediate execution on toggle — turning Autorun ON mid-day triggers no immediate orders. This prevents unintended trades on stale intraday data and gives the user time to reconsider before the next market open.
